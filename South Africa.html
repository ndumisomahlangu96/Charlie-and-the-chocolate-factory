South Africa

Capital:The capital city of South Africa is Pretoria. However, Cape Town is the
legislative capital, and Bloemfontein is the judicial capital.

Major Cities:Besides Pretoria, Cape Town, and Bloemfontein, other major cities
include Johannesburg, Durban, and Port Elizabeth.

Official Languages:South Africa has 11 official languages, reflecting its diverse
cultural landscape. These languages include Afrikaans, English, isiNdebele, isiXhosa,
isiZulu, Sesotho, Setswana, siSwati, Tshivenda, Xitsonga, and Sepedi.

Government:South Africa is a constitutional democracy with a parliamentary
system. The President is both the head of state and the head of government.

History:South Africa has a complex history marked by colonialism and apartheid.
The system of apartheid, a policy of racial segregation and discrimination, was
dismantled in the early 1990s, leading to the establishment of a democratic and
multiracial government.

Economy:South Africa has a diverse economy, with sectors such as mining,
agriculture, manufacturing, and services playing significant roles. It is rich in natural
resources, including gold, platinum, and diamonds.

Tourism: South Africa is known for its stunning landscapes, wildlife, and cultural
diversity. Tourist attractions include Kruger National Park, Table Mountain, Robben
Island, and the Garden Route.

Wildlife:The country is famous for its diverse wildlife, including the "Big Five" (lion,
elephant, buffalo, leopard, and rhinoceros). Kruger National Park is one of the
premier safari destinations in the world.

Sports:South Africa has a strong sporting culture, and sports such as rugby, cricket,
and soccer are widely followed. The country hosted the 2010 FIFA World Cup, which
was a significant event in its history.
